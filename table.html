Ahilyabai Holkar Born in 1725 in Chaundi, Maharashtra, she became the ruler of Malwa and led with justice and wisdom.
  • Rebuilt temples, including Kashi Vishwanath Temple.
  • Developed roads, wells, and shelters for the public.
  • Encouraged trade and agriculture.
 Leadership, social responsibility, and dedication to public welfare.
Rani Lakshmibai Born in 1828 in Varanasi, she became the queen of Jhansi and a fearless leader in the 1857 rebellion.
  • Fought against British rule to protect Jhansi.
  • Inspired women to take part in freedom struggles.
 Courage, determination, and patriotism.
Savitribai Phule Born in 1831 in Maharashtra, she was India's first female teacher and a pioneer in women's education.
  • Established the first school for girls in India.
  • Fought against caste and gender discrimination.
 Importance of education, social justice, and perseverance.

PV Sindhu Born in 1995 in Hyderabad, she is a world-class badminton player and Olympic medalist.

  • Inspired young athletes in India.

MS Subbulakshmi Born in 1916 in Tamil Nadu, she was a legendary Carnatic singer and the first musician to receive Bharat Ratna.
  • Revived and promoted Indian classical music.
  • Performed at the United Nations.
 Passion, dedication to art, and cultural pride.
Saalumarada Thimmakka Born around 1910 in Karnataka, she is an environmentalist who planted over 8,000 trees.
  • Recognized for afforestation efforts.
  • Inspired eco-conservation movements.
 Commitment to nature, patience, and sustainability.
Lakshmi Sahgal Born in 1914, she was a freedom fighter and leader in the Indian National Army.
  • Led the Rani of Jhansi Regiment.
  • Fought for women's empowerment.
 Patriotism, resilience, and leadership.
Lata Mangeshkar Born in 1929 in Indore, she was one of India's greatest playback singers, known as the Nightingale of India.
  • Recorded thousands of songs in multiple languages.
  • Honored with Bharat Ratna and international recognition.
 Perseverance, passion, and humility.
Marie Curie Born in 1867 in Poland, she was a physicist and chemist who discovered radioactivity.
  • First woman to win a Nobel Prize.
  • Discovered radium and polonium.
 Scientific curiosity, dedication, and persistence.
