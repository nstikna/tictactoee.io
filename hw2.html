Powerful Women In Indian History

Personality Name About

Ahilyabai Holkar

Ahilyabai Holkar was born in 1725 in Chaundi, Maharashtra. She became the ruler of Malwa and was known for her just and wise governance. She built temples, roads, and wells across India. She believed in righteousness and often said, “Charity and duty are my religion.”

Rani Laxmibai

Rani Lakshmibai was born in 1828 in Varanasi. She was a fearless warrior who fought against British rule in 1857. She refused to surrender her kingdom and is remembered for saying, “Main meri Jhansi nahi dungi!”

Savitribai Phule

Savitribai Phule was born in 1831 in Maharashtra. She was India’s first female teacher and fought for women’s education. She dedicated her life to social reform, saying, “Awake, arise and educate!”.

Yesubai

Yesubai was a Maratha queen and wife of Sambhaji Maharaj. She bravely defended her son’s claim to the throne against the Mughals. She is remembered for her courage and sacrifice.

PV Sindhu

PV Sindhu was born in 1995 in Hyderabad. She is an Olympic medal-winning badminton player and a symbol of dedication in sports. Her success proves, “Hard work never fails.”

MS Subbulaxmi

MS Subbulakshmi was born in 1916 in Tamil Nadu. She was a legendary Carnatic singer and the first Indian musician to receive the Bharat Ratna. She believed, “Music is divine.”

Saalumarada Thimmakka

Saalumarada Thimmakka was born around 1910 in Karnataka. She is an environmentalist known for planting thousands of trees. She inspires with her message, “Protect nature, it will protect you.”

Lakshmi Sahgal

Lakshmi Sahgal was born in 1914 in India. She was a freedom fighter and leader in the Indian National Army. She fought for women’s empowerment and said,“Women can be warriors too.”


Lata Mangeshkar

Lata Mangeshkar was born in 1929 in Indore. She was one of India’s greatest singers, known as the Nightingale of India. She believed, “Music is the voice of the soul.”

Marie Curie

Marie Curie was born in 1867 in Poland. She was a scientist who discovered radioactivity and won two Nobel Prizes. Her words,“Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood,” continue to inspire scientists today.
